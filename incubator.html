Stereographic Reconstruction of 3-D Landscape Geometry from Airborne Imagery


Presented by

Hong Chen

NASA Airborne Imagery


Stereographic Reconstruction


Preliminary Results



Summary and Future Work


1. The prelimary study of Cloud Top Height (CTH) has shown that the altitude of an object can be derived from successive airborne images using stereographic reconstruction.


2. The bias study and error analysis will be performed through the comparison between the estimated CTH and the direct measurements of CTH from other airborne instruments.


3. Object detection and object tracking algorithms, e.g., Principal Component Analysis (PCA), will be applied to automatically identify and track objects from successive images.
